Research

Methodology and Pipeline Architecture for Explainable Multilingual Hate Speech Analysis

Problem Statement

Hate speech on social media platforms has become a critical challenge, particularly in multilingual contexts where regional languages are prevalent. Traditional hate speech detection systems often lack explainability and fail to capture the nuanced nature of hate narratives across different languages and cultural contexts.

Justify.AI addresses three key challenges:

  • Multilingual Detection: Supporting Indian regional languages including Hindi, Bhojpuri, Marathi, and Marwari alongside English
  • Explainability: Providing transparent insights into why content is classified as hate speech through lexical, causal, and bias analysis
  • Comprehensive Analysis: Going beyond binary classification to understand the structure and dimensions of hate narratives

Methodology

Our approach combines multiple NLP techniques to provide comprehensive hate speech analysis:

1. Lexicon-Based Detection

We employ automated lexicon discovery to identify hate speech terms across multiple languages. The system uses word embeddings and semantic similarity to build comprehensive lexicons with severity scoring based on contextual usage patterns.

2. Causal Narrative Extraction

Using dependency parsing and semantic role labeling, we extract cause-and-effect relationships within hate narratives. This reveals how different elements of hate speech connect and reinforce each other, providing insights into the structure of harmful discourse.

3. Bias Quantification

We measure bias across multiple dimensions including religion, gender, and ethnicity using specialized bias lexicons and contextual embeddings. Each dimension is scored independently to provide a comprehensive bias profile.

4. Risk Scoring

A composite risk score (0-100) is calculated by combining lexical severity, causal narrative complexity, and bias intensity. This provides a single metric for assessing the overall harmfulness of content while maintaining transparency through component breakdowns.

Pipeline Stages

  1. Text Preprocessing: Language detection, tokenization, and normalization
  2. Lexicon Matching: Identification of hate speech terms with severity scoring
  3. Causal Extraction: Dependency parsing and relationship extraction
  4. Bias Analysis: Multi-dimensional bias quantification
  5. Risk Scoring: Composite score calculation and result aggregation

Datasets

Our system is trained and evaluated on multiple datasets covering different languages and contexts:

English Hate Speech Dataset

Curated collection of labeled hate speech examples from social media platforms, covering multiple bias dimensions including race, religion, and gender.

Size: ~50,000 samples

Hindi-English Code-Mixed Dataset

Code-mixed content from Twitter and Facebook, representing real-world multilingual communication patterns common in Indian social media.

Size: ~30,000 samples

Regional Language Corpus

Annotated datasets for Bhojpuri, Marathi, and Marwari, enabling analysis of hate speech in low-resource Indian regional languages.

Size: ~20,000 samples across languages

Evaluation Metrics

We evaluate system performance using multiple metrics to ensure comprehensive assessment:

Classification Metrics

  • Precision: Proportion of correctly identified hate speech among all flagged content
  • Recall: Proportion of actual hate speech correctly identified by the system
  • F1-Score: Harmonic mean of precision and recall, providing balanced performance measure
  • Accuracy: Overall correctness across all classifications

Explainability Metrics

  • Lexicon Coverage: Percentage of hate speech instances explained by lexicon matches
  • Causal Graph Coherence: Semantic consistency of extracted causal relationships
  • Bias Attribution Accuracy: Correctness of bias dimension assignments
